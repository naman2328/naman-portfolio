Vision Systems in Industrial Automation

In industrial environments, vision systems are not judged by accuracy alone. They are judged by determinism, timing predictability, and how reliably they communicate decisions to automation logic.

Why Vision Systems Fail on the Factory Floor

  • Lighting variation and reflections
  • Part vibration and inconsistent positioning
  • Non-deterministic inference latency
  • Unstable OK / NG signaling to PLCs

Most failures are not algorithmic — they are system-level failures.

Design Philosophy

Determinism > Raw Accuracy
Any uncertainty, timing violation, or ambiguous result must fail safe (NG).
  • Fixed cycle-time budgets
  • Binary decisions only (OK / NG)
  • Signal latching compatible with PLC scan cycles
  • No asynchronous ambiguity
